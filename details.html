My Favorite Plants

Welcome to the details section of my greenhouse journey, where I’ll introduce some of my favorite plants and share how I care for them. Over the years, I’ve experimented with a variety of species, each with its own unique needs. One of the most stunning plants in my collection is the Agave Victoriae-Reginae, also known as Queen Victoria Agave. This succulent is known for its symmetrical rosette of thick leaves with striking white markings, and it requires minimal watering, making it a perfect low-maintenance plant. I water it sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out completely between waterings.

The Golden Barrel Cactus is another favorite. Its spherical shape and golden-yellow spines make it a standout in my greenhouse. This cactus loves sunlight and thrives with only occasional watering. I’ve found that too much water can be harmful, so I keep its care routine simple.

My Agave Americana and Agave Marginata are the giants of the greenhouse, each known for their large, pointed leaves. These plants love warmth and sunlight, but I’ve learned to keep their roots dry to prevent rot.

Finally, my Jade Plant (Crassula ovata) has been one of the easiest to care for. Its thick, fleshy leaves store water, allowing it to thrive even when I forget to water it occasionally.

  • Agave Victoriae-Reginae (Queen Victoria Agave)
    A striking succulent known for its symmetrical rosette of thick leaves marked with white lines.
  • Golden Barrel Cactus (Echinocactus grusonii)
    A spherical cactus famous for its golden-yellow spines and distinct round shape.
  • Agave Americana
    This large succulent has fleshy, pointed leaves and can grow to impressive sizes.
  • Agave Marginata (Variegated Agave)
    A variegated species of agave with beautiful white-edged leaves.
  • Jade Plant (Crassula ovata)
    A popular succulent known for its thick, fleshy leaves and low maintenance care needs.
